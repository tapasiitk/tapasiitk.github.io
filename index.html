About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur, India, specializing in cognitive science and decision-making research. My current work explores the influence of context effects on decision-making and the role of cognitive biases in shaping human judgment.

With a background in Electronics and Instrumentation Engineering, I bring an interdisciplinary perspective to cognitive science, combining experimental design, statistical analysis, and computational modeling.

Research

PhD Research

I work on context effects in decision-making, exploring how different options influence choices. I primarily use vanilla JavaScript for both offline and online experiments and rely on Python and R for statistics, data analysis, and visualization.

Current Projects

  • Investigating the Intention-Action Selection Link in the Sense of Agency within Recommender Systems.
    Studying how recommendations influence users' perceived control and decision-making.

Future Directions

  • Sense of Agency in Agentic AI
    Exploring how people perceive their control when working with autonomous AI systems.
  • Player Sense of Agency in Multiplayer Online Games
    Understanding decision-making and agency in interactive digital environments.
